Biography

My passion for technology began when I got my first computer (Windows 95!) at the tender age of 4. During middle school, I delved into HTML and CCS, creating websites and forums. I even attempted my first videogame prototype with RPG Maker, allowing me to gain hands-on experience with Ruby scripting. However, it was in high school, at the technical institute “E. Fermi - R. Guttuso”, that I began studying Computer Science, learning the fundamentals of programming, networking, and databases.

In 2016, immediately after high school, I enrolled at the University of Catania to pursue my Computer Science studies. I earned my bachelor’s degree in 2019 with the thesis “Smart Hub and 3DCP - Dynamic Domotic Device”, for which I received full marks, under the supervision of prof. Corrado Santoro and prof. Federico Santoro. Continuing my studies, I pursued a master’s degree in Security and Network Systems. During my Master’s degree, I broadened my horizons through the Erasmus program at University College Dublin (Ireland) for a semester. I also interned at Robert Bosch GmbH (Germany), where I worked on my first article “Embedded fuzzing: a review of challenges, tools, and solutions”, , published in the Springer Cybersecurity journal. In 2021, I worked on my master thesis, titled “Shared Memory Fuzzing”, at the Bosch Campus for Research and Advance Engineering in Renningen. Once again, I graduated with a final mark of 110/110 cum laude.

While working on my master thesis, I came across the article “Fuzzing: Challenges and Reflections”, which inspired my PhD research project. Currently, I am researching ways to enhance the automation of fuzzing in terms of applicability over complex systems. Specifically, I am intrigued by the idea of employing fuzzing on multiprocess systems and web servers, from embedded devices to cloud APIs. I am pursuing my PhD studies at the University of Catania since the end of 2021, in the Network and Security Informatics research group led by prof. Giampaolo Bella, who serves as my supervisor. Last but not least, I am delighted to be on a research visit at King’s College London under the guidance of Prof. Fabio Pierazzi.

28 Sep 2023
Forkfuzz

Forkfuzz is the first fork-aware fuzzer. It is built on top of Honggfuzz, one of the most popular and effec- tive coverage-guided fuzzers, as reported by the Fuzzbench benchmark. To show its effectiveness, we tested our fuzzer over two classical pro- gramming problems: the Dining Philosophers Problem and a version of the Producer-Consumer Problem where the consumer (the child) process crashes for specific inputs. Furthermore, we evaluated Forkfuzz against a real and more complex scenario involving an HTTP server that handles multiple connections through multiple processes. The results of our eval- uation demonstrate the effectiveness of Forkfuzz in identifying crashes and timeouts
