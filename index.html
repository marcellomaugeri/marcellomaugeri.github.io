Biography

Born in 1997 in Giarre (CT), I had my first computer with Windows 95 when I was only four years old. I guess my interest for technology started from there. After the middle school I embraced videogame developing by creating my first videogame prototype with RPG maker. I then started to study Computer Science during the highschool at the technical institute “E. Fermi - R. Guttuso” where I learned the basics of programming, networking and databases.

In 2016, right after the highschool, I immediately enrolled at the University of Catania to pursue my Computer Science studies. I got my bachelor degree in 2019 with the thesis “Smart Hub and 3DCP - Dynamic Domotic Device” thanks to which I got full marks. Subsequently, I continued my studies with a master degree in Security and Network Systems. During my Master’s degree I had the opportunity to broaden my horizons by participating in the Erasmus programme at University College Dublin (Ireland) for a semester. I also had the opportunity to do an internship in Robert Bosch GmbH (Germany) where I worked on my first article “Embedded fuzzing: a review of challenges, tools, and solutions”, published in Springer Cybersecurity journal.In 2021, I had the opportunity to work on my master thesis, with title “Shared Memory Fuzzing”, in the Bosch Campus for Research and Advance Engineering in Renningen. Again, I graduated with a final mark of 110/110 cum laude.

Now I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Catania from the end of 2021. I am currently researching on improving the automation of fuzzing in terms of applicability. I am also interested in the IoT Security and Quantum Security fields. I am a member of the Network and Security Informatics research group led by prof. Giampaolo Bella, which is my supervisor.
